I originally build this build for a good rp example for a Ko'di Blademaster (see Avendesora's Ko'di Blademaster) for the Wheel of Time - The Third Age server, because he can achive a high AB while still staying in cloth armor. On this server, as I've said before, the gear is very faction driven, and within the faction that this build is in, the Hundredman rank(epic-1) Has cloth armor with a +6 ac, so I didnt feel that sacraficing an ab, and 4 fortitude points was worth it. (Considering a 60 AC) He does have a decent hide, so in the right hide gear, he can stay out of sight of most players. You could also get appraise and spot instead (As I done on the server). 60 ac, and 60 ab makes him a fearsom opponent to any but the best Str WM's and the intense Munchkin builds, and even then, a formidable opponent. When in duel, Ghostly Visage can be a big help.

I'd love to hear any suggestions to improve this build, or your thoughts. Also, corrections, if I've made a mistake are well recived.

Daryl By trying to do both str and dex you end up with a character that is gimped. Yes, I am sure that for what you have outlined, it would do fine, but your dex isnt really high enough to do both. You are trying to be a dexer (wearing cloth) yet you come up short on the "ONLY" dexer feat, Epic dodge. Without this, your cloth will hurt you. IF you want to do this type of thing, go bard/rdd/rog, take Elf and pump the heck out of dex, while allowing the rdd lvls to make up for DC.

Other than that, if you are happy with the result, then Oh, and thanks for posting it here, as it is a PW specific build. Calling a build which doesn't even utilize Weapon Finesse a Dexer is, IMHO, a bit misleading. Anyway I agree with the previous poster on everything he said, really.

Nevertheless, it seems that you have succeeded in what you were trying to get. However, there are some tweaks I'd make, such as swapping Rogue over Assassin, and managing your Epic Feats better (namely, pick EWF on a WM bonus feats so you can get another two Great Attribute feats by dropping Epic Fortitude). You'd lose Epic Toughness II; good riddance!
