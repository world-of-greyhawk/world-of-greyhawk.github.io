Torbel sat at the table by the window. He didn’t care much for the smoke that filled the small room, and he welcomed the gentle breeze that blew in the window. Two drinks were on the table, but no-one sat with him; a small cup of a hot, dark and bitter liquid and small glass containing a clear alcoholic syrup, it tasted of somewhat liquorice. He enjoyed the way the two flavours danced and competed with each other as he drank them. As he relaxed he listened to the chatter of the inn.

”It was him I tell ye!” The exclamation came from a noisy Dwarf close by. ”Eidolon it was, not a word of a lie.” The Dwarf’s acquaintance scoffed. ”Hims just stood there bold as brass. Talking to that nasty piece o’ work Grunak he was. I was watching, made Grunak pay him double …. fer something or other.”. The Dwarf was getting agitated. ”Pah! Higard, you spout too much bile, no ones ever seen Eidolon, he’s a myth.”

Torbel smiled wryly, Eidolon so that’s what they called him. Grunak … he remembered the burly half-Orc and his henchmen. He had wanted him to fulfil a contract. It was a simple enough task that had warranted more money than Grunak was prepared to pay. Grunak was all talk though; even though Torbel was not a particular tall man he was strong and well toned, he had always had the ability to impose himself on people, they could see the rage that burned inside him. Grunak had paid him more, it was that or die.

”They say he appears from nowhere, and can pull the darkness in around him even on the brightest of days” Higard continued excitedly. “I know Higard” was the bored response. “Just to look into his eyes can paralyze you with fear … you can’t be looking at him ... tis certain death”. Higard blurted noisly, spitting beer over his companion. “Hey!” shouted the barman, ”I told you before Dwarf, keep it down … now get out!”. Higard and his companion begrudginly got up from the table and headed for the door ”Well done Higard, that’s another Inn we can’t go in to.” muttered Vincent.

Torbel didn’t care much for people knowing his face, especially if he hadn’t put the fear of God into them first. He threw down some coin to pay for his drinks and anonymously followed to two companions outside. He followed them for a short while until they were on the road out of town, the two were arguing with each other incessantly. Torbel picked up his pace and caught up with the two men easily.

”Afternoon gents”. ”Wha? Hey? Who are you?” replied Vincent. Torbel took a deep breath and calmly replied ”Why … I am Eidolon”. Torbel channelled the rage the burned at his inner being and brought it to aide him. The two journey men stood staring at him, transfixed, paralyzed by the fear that burned in his eyes. Torbel pulled a dagger from his side and with effortless flashes of his blade made incisive cuts to his foes major blood vessels and organs. The Dwarf and his companion dropped lifelessly to the road side.

Torbel cleaned his blade, sheathed it and moved on, leaving the bodies were they lay. He was a killer, but he didn’t care much for sneaking around like a common thief … he preferred to look his mark in the eye, the fear on their face fuelled his inner rage, their death breathed life into him.
