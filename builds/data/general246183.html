Well in 2nd ed., males could not be clerics and females could not be wizards/sorcerers. Among the drow the clerics of Lolth are feared, and among their enemies the wizards and sorcerers are probably feared more. Hence while drow society is matriarchal, the wizards are more or less left to their own devices and granted a degree of autonomy. If you don't want females bossing you around all the time, I suggest you consider a wizard or sorcerer as a primary class (you might even organize the other wizards and sorcerers and stage a revolt as the denizens of Chad Nassar did). I would avoid clerics, paladins, druids, blackguards, rangers (the drow have souts but they are more likely fighter/rogues than rangers) and champions of torm as they are divine classes and unlikely for a male. Because drow society is in an almost constant cold war state where each house is trying to weaken the other houses. Rogue and assassin would be good choices too. Because many arguements are settled by duels, fighter and weapon master would good choices too.

Hence I suggest a pure wizard or pure sorcerer, followed by a wizard/rogue (which can be done fairly well in 30 levels), a wizard/AA, a fighter/rogue or a fighter/WM.

Hope that helps.
