Ok that the task make me proud First of all, you realize that you can't Finesse scimitars, which means that this will have to be a Str-based build. Generally speaking, one of the best ways to add AC to a Str-based build is to wear a shield, but since you're dual-wielding, no shield for you. Also realize that you will suffer a -4 penalty to hit even with all the dual-wield feats because scimitars are not considered light weapons.

If you don't mind being cheesy, I'd suggest something like a Bard 14/RDD 10/PM 16, because he can get a high AC and still manage to do decent damage. You'll get +8 AC from Tumble, +4 from RDD, and another +6 from PM. You will also get +3 Dodge AC from Bard Song, which stacks with Dodge AC from items.

In addition, you can cast Haste on yourself for +4 AC, Epic Mage Armor should boost your AC by another 20 points, Cat's Grace will boost it by another 1-2 points (1-4 if it's empowered), and then you have Improved Invis., which doesn't improve your AC, but which WILL give your opponents a %50 chance to miss every time they attack you (25% miss chance with Blind-Fight).

So, assuming you're wearing mundane medium armor, your AC should look something like this:

10 base
+4 Dex
+8 Tumble
+4 RDD
+10 PM
+3 Dodge (Bard Song)
+4 Haste
+20 Epic Mage Armor
+4 Medium Armor
+2 Armor Skin

Note that this is your AC without items of any kind.
