I haven't made a build yet, but I have some sketchy thoughts.

The AB and damage increase of AA come at odd numbers. So it can be wise not to end up with an even number of AA levels.

Bard has a wide selection of class skills, so it should be easy to plan the skill progression.

You get turn undead at BG lvl 3. If you can get a divine feat at the lvl you get turn undead (correct me if I'm wrong), you don't need more than 3 BG lvls to take e.g. divine might. I think DM is better than DS in an archer build.
Have you considered taking Cleric to get the divine feats?

The Bull's from BG and Bard stack and give you a nice damage bonus with mighty bows. You need str 13 to get cleave (for BG)

I'd take bard at least to lvl 20 for LI and have at least Cha 16. If you buff with eagle's and carry Cha items, Divine might can give you a nice extra against otherwise immune opponents.
If you intend to rely more heavily on the divine feats, I'd take Cha higher. That will also make a better saves bonus if you take BG lvls.

You can use both elf and half elf. Elf has better Dex but worse Con. If you take BG before AA, the half elf will get weapon prof martial and can take WF longbow to qualify for AA.

example feat progression (lvl 1-8 Bard)
1 Bard, point blank shot
3 Bard, power attack
6 Bard, cleave
9 BG, WF longbow
10 AA
