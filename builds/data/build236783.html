Among the followers of Chauntea, the most widespread church in all of Faerûn, there exists a holy order of knights that seeks to protect the fields from every sort of blight. Paladins of the Order of the Field Guardians are charged with seeking out those who would wish ruin on the crops of her followers.

This is an attempt to make a "turning" machine with excellent offensive and defensive powers, able to use a massive charisma bonus and high cleric levels to turn (or destroy instantly):

Damage output per kama hit with no magic items: 1d6 + 4 STR (Divine Power) + 5 (Greater Magic Weapon) + 9 (Divine Might) + 7 magical (+5 Divine Favor, +2 Battletide) + 1d6+10 fire (Darkfire). Minimum damage: 37 per hit, of which 17 cannot be prevented by most targets (divine and magic damage). It's even more fun when you add the divine and magic damage to ranged damage with Zen Archery and a magic bow (which is what the Field Guardian uses from levels 18 on; before that, a morningstar).

Thus far we have a pretty balanced character offensively and defensively. Not superb at either, but far above average for a support character and still better than most at both. Tack on the full power of cleric healing, and a spell resistance of 50 (that can be doled out to companions), and you have a versatile healer than can help out anywhere on the battlefield. More than enough cleric levels to take a few Harm spells with you.

So, why on earth would I take Plant and Earth domains, and what's up with the planar turning? Hehe, well, those are two of Chauntea's domains, and they both add to turning ability, where this build can shine. This cleric will turn as a Level 42 cleric half the time, due to her high CHA. She'll also be able to turn up to a 64 HD of undead, vermin, outsiders, or elementals (19 times per day). Outsiders add their full spell resistance to their Hit Dice for turn attempts. Planar turning cuts this in half (and if your Sorcerer friend happens to hit it with a Mord's Disjunction, that's effectively +5 to your turn check).

Plant domain is useful throughout the lower levels, and even into the higher levels, as it allows the cleric to maintain +3 to +5 Natural Armor bonus while wearing a Periapt of Wisdom. There are a lot of vermin on the way up, and even some epic vermin HotU. Earth Domain is less important, and infact Destruction Domain is better (do approximately 100 unavoidable damage a pop to Constructs), but Earth is a Chauntea domain and I'm working on an Umberlant version (with Blackguard instead) that will use Water and Destruction to the same effect.
