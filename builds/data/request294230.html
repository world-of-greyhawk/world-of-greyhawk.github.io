A Role Play, Low Magic world Druid 25/Shifter 13/something 2 gives you 2 bonus epic feats and a decent caster lvl (plus undispellable buffs) but you give up on wildshape III and humanoid improved forms (and on infinite wildshape iv). For th esomething part: if not monk, then rogue.

Druid 21-22/Shifter 17/something 1-2 for epic shapes, 2 epic bonus feats (as above) at the cost of caster lvl and undispellability. Same something as above.

In both builds you can get at least 1 epic spell: Hellball fits the character concept, but it's otherwise no big deal unless beefed up.

I'd go for the second build if you want both some caster power and all shapes. I'd also go with Monk, cause a +10 to AC plus tumble ranks is difficult to pass, if not just impossible.

A Role Play, Low Magic world A druid shifter blackguard would have demonic and undead servitors in addition to the odd shapes he takes, and if built right would have taunt, high wisdom and another mental stat that would increase damage and ab in any form. Also, from an rp perspective you dont want to forget the rabbi who built the first golem. The history of the golem worldwide is one of inanimate objects moved by unclean spirits. You might also think about Druid Monk Assassin and use the Elder Earth elemental shape.

Your build would have base 34 Str and 22 Con when shifted. Your theme could be "Demonic Elemental Lord." Dumping all of your level up stat points into Wisdom would net you more monk ac.

26/2/12 for unlimited Elemental shape.
20/2/18 for 3 shapes but much more Death Attack damage.

I have played that build on BoW and like it alot.

You also have the option of using Air Elemental shape for 34 Dex instead. That would increase your AC and sneak ability. Weapon Finness yourself for some nice AB too.

I suggest taking 2 monk levels for the Deflect Arrow freebee. It's worth it's weight in gold if you ask me.

I also suggest more assassin levels since your death attack DC will be higher.
