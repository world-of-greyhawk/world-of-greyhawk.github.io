I was intrigued by his build and wondered how it would turn out as a strength-based brutal thug...thus was born the Thug of Torm.

"You cannot pass!" I think there are 2 reasons to get his Dex to 16. One is to give his Rogue skills a fighting chance. The other is to give him a choice in Armors to wear. If the situation warrants, and he has the appropriate items/buffs, he should be able to get the same AC in padded armor or Robes as he would in Full plate. This is exactly how I prefer to make my Assassins: strength-based but yet still a small commitment to Dexterity to provide for ultimate flexibility at all levels.

I am flattered, Kaliban. Once again it is demonstrated with facts and figures that Dwarves make the best Rogues. My only suggestion in a strength-version would be to squeeze in some Stealth skill foci somewhere, perhaps in dropping the Disarm or Expertise feats, and sticking in some ESFs in place of Slippery and Epic Toughness.

Christian, thanks for the compliments, but full credit goes to the Dwarflord.

As for how to play this character, I'm actually playing him in the HotU OC right now (Nostalgia called, what can I say?) and he's brutally efficient. I'm playing him as a plate-wearing tank with rogue skills -- I wanted a lock picker/trap disarmer with the nifty Rogue bonus skills and feats (Tumble, UMD, Sneak Attack, Crippling Strike, etc). For that reason I neglected to bring up his stealth skills - my Thug isn't quite as refined as Grizz's Rogue o' War. As Grizz suggests (and he created in his build) flexibility is attained with the 16 Dex and then Epic Skill Focii in Hide and Move Silently.

On a PW or with this character in a different role in another single-player Mod I would follow Grizz's advice and build in the stealth skills as he advises. Actually, after reading Grizz's response above, I decided that after I finish the OC -- if I choose to play this character in other epic mods -- I will de-level him and relevel him in a trainer, taking advantage of the advice in Grizz's post.

For full details on why a Dex of 16, read Grizz's excellent post and playing guide for The Deathstroke (assassin): Click Here
