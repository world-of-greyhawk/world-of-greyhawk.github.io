You realize your stat spread is going to be incredibly thin, don't you?

WM requires 13 DEX and 13 INT. If you want any use at all out of Cleric, you'll need WIS of 12+. You'll also want STR. I just don't see this as viable at all for a 1/2 Orc.

You'll also find the feat requirements rather steep. WM requires 6 feats and you only get 7 Pre-epic with the 1/2 Orc build.

Another consideration is: if you want Bane, that's 21 Ranger levels. WM would be (I assume) minimum of 7 levels. That leaves 12 levels for Cleric. Not all that bad for a levels spread, but you'll need 16 WIS to support those CLC levels. This means less STR in the build.

Why CLC? Why WM? Which do you think would be more important (I don't think both are viable).

Ranger 35/FTR 5, STR-based. Forget WM. You'll have +8+2d6 vs FEs (and you can take a lot of them) +6 (WS/EWS) + STR modifier (probably 30 base STR, possibly 32) for a base of:

24 (or 25) + 2d6 + weapon base + enhancements

damage per hit.

If you really want to cheese out, take Club as your weapon and go with RGR 35/FTR 2/Rogue 3. You can still get WS/EWS in Club (it's a quirk with Club that you can take WS with only 1 FTR level, as long as your BAB is +4 or higher). This gives you better AC (Tumble as a class skill), Evasion, and 2d6 sneak damage.
