The second installment in my short series of Dexterity based Paladin builds is quite different from the first. This build is meant to kill pretty much anything that moves. It has an effective 76ab (for a short duration, with Taunt and Divine Wrath), Holy Sword, Epic Dodge, UMD, very high saves, Epic Traps, and can deal out a ton of damage for a Dex build. High listen and modal detect cancel hipsers, while 7d6 sneaks add some extra wallop to your attacks. Be prepared for an angry backlash of tells, as you will frustrate people with this build in PvP &#61514;

Also, with your stout saves and Holy Sword, you have little to fear from mages. Enjoy!

Pro’s: Holy Sword, High AB and AC, Traps, UMD, Taunt, Sneak Attacks, Epic Dodge, Divine Wrath… did I mention Holy Sword?

Con’s: /Recurring Theme: Dex build vulnerability to Iron Horn.

Strategy: Ethereal Visage scrolls to counter Iron Horn (see a pattern here?). Flame Weapon scrolls. Improved Invisibility scrolls. Pretty much any scroll that helps, really. Cast Holy Sword, knock stuff down, and hit it until its dead… repeat as necessary. Lay a few traps to lure opponents into in case you need to flee. Taunt your opponent… then Taunt them a second time!!! Save Divine Wrath for the when you really need it. Stock Power Word scrolls for runners, and away you go!

Notes: Why Elf? High Dexterity and Modal Detect. Why Hand Axe? Better critical multiplier (on my PW, crits around 130ish). Why Expertise? Boost your AC by 5… and still have an AB of 60! Why Taunt? WHY NOT!?!? Paladins combine not only some of the best AB buffs with excellent BAB progression, AND they can Taunt opponents to lower their AC. All in all, a deadly combination.
