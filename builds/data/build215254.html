I used this build a few months ago on a heavy RP server, I RP'ed him as a Goblin named Waagh. But recently I checked the index to see if I could find a build like it, and... I did! But I checked again and found nothing so here is Waagh the Goblin.

"Waagh, thats my name, I think?" he ponders, a tendril of drool runs down his lip and splatters noisely on the wooden floor of the inn. The dark man before him stares, inquisitively, at the little man; he draws a deep breath from the end of his pipe then exhaling the smoke in a ring around the goblins head."What brings you here, little one?" he asks, the goblins face swells with redness and water fills his round eyes. "Uh uhm.. Waagh comes from far place," he points off into the forest which is viewable from the nearby window. "Great magician keep Waagh in his home, all day." the goblin looks at his feet and hands, his hair was singed slightly and a foul odor came from them. "What happened there?" the man asked the pitiful creature. There was a long pause, several smoke rings flew gracefully into the air before the goblin responded. "Waagh ran away, ofcourse..." Behind him a crackle, a sound of thunder, could be heard in the distance. The forest exploded in a fury of flame. Evil spirits ran through the town, sacking and pillaging the town. The goblin scurried off into the cellar, but something fell from his pocket. He fell noiselessly onto the ground, and the man went to inspect it. "Dear God," he fingered the parchment carefully, then ripped it viciously into a thousand fragments. The area surrounding him began to glow a deep red, and a hum could be heard in the distance. He looked out the window, the glass had melted, and saw the silhouette of a man, clothed in fire, in the distance. The silhouette pulled a scroll quickly from the mist. The man recognized it instantly, it was the same one he had just destroyed. He ran for cover, but it was too late. A fireball, burning with the intensity of a thousand suns, came smashing mercilessly into the inn and circling the spot where the man once stood. An explosion envelopped the small hamlet in a horrific hellball of heat and energy.
Already in the distance the little figure of Waagh could be seen, running faster than he did at the last town, and the remaining skin falling off of his left arm....
