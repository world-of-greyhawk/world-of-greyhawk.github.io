Thax pretty much summarized it, but I'll add a bit as well:

ECL stands for Effective Character Level. This is applied to all non-full BAB classes after level 1 and up to level 8 for most, level 14 for Bards. Basically, since the game is so combat heavy, the designers decided these classes needed a little help at lower levels, so what happens is when the game is dishing out XP for kills, it looks at your classes. If you're playing one of the 'weaker' classes (and those classes are in a certain level range), the game will treat your character as being lower level than he actually is when calculating the XP awards.

The adjustment is not linear though. At level 1, all classes get the same XP per kill. It starts to change at level 2, but some classes have a wider gap than others for a while. Somewhere around in the archived threads there's a chart, but suffice to say Bard is the way to go if you're strictly looking at levelling fast.

Actually, the way to go for the fastest would be Cleric 5/SOR 5/Bard X). This will get you a major adjustment in XP. I believe it was Blue Emu who posted this mix. Playing through the OC with this combo, he was level 14 or 15 at the end of chapter one (normally you're around level 9, IIRC).
