Introduction:
Whisper of the Forest

Ranger/Bard/HS -- a theme/RP build (yes ). Decent Bard Song, Hide/MS in 60s when buffed, decent AB, AC is fairly weak but decent when DEX is buffed. Damage output is respectable, in testing I killed a 15/- DR opponent without much trouble. Great selection of skills, too few skill points. But hey, it's always like that, isn't it?

very nicely done. i am a fan of rangers, and harper scouts. this was very nicely put together. the addition of bard, HS and ranger together comes out with a strong melee presence.

the only points i wish to make are as follows. they are minor, and purely technical.

you don't have heavy armour, so you need to drop a feat in exchange for picking this up. i would have to say knockdown or epic fort would be the ones to go. i was tempted to say empower spell, but the usefulness of empowering bulls is kinda important. even and empowered cats is still useful, although with the HS cats its not as necessary in heavy armour (between the two, empowered cats loses its importance).
also, if going this route, dropping dex to 8. two cats will get you to 12 at least (enough for plate) and then put the extra points into con and int. this also nets you more HP and skillpoints. you can then max taunt, which would give you another advantage. i would stick with the single weapon and shield, even in heavy amour, to give you a really strong tank. a taunting, singing, tank striking from the shadows, with extra strength and 50% concealment, only retreating to let the enemy fall into traps and allow the panther companion (buffed with bulls, cats, mage armour, improv vis, greater magic fang, haste and aid) to rack up some crazy sneak damage. now that is a fight is just brutal.

i do like the two weapon aspect, but when you need a good tank this one good put up a good fight for quite some time. very impressive. also, i thought this combo was done before, but i cannot find it. with that being the case, the congrats must be piled a little higher.

well done.

-c

ps: a bard can cast all arcane spells from scrolls without any UMD, for those who didn't know. this makes this chars job even easier, and can even lead to being a sort of simple group buffer using many scrolls of bulls, cats, endurance, impr invis, gmw, flame weapon, etc. adding a little flame damage to your pet isn't a bad idea either. the possibilities just keep adding up with this build. buffing a bear with stoneskin woudl make a nice meat shield for the dual-wielding version of this build. the more i think about this, the more i like it. -cs
