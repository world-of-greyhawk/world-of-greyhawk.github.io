Colonel Addams regrouped his troops. They were wounded and in disarray, but somehow ready. The attackers had withdrawn to regroup after their desperate defense, but he knew the next assault would be the fatal. Their enemies were only regrouping to maximise their efficiency, and minimise losses. All of the defenses were mostly broken, and most of his soldiers wounded or dead. They refreshed themselves and re-equipped. They bandaged up the wounded and made them ready for the last stand, hoping to buy enough time for his townspeople to get away, though in his heart he knew they wouldn't. Their town was the trade center of the region, and much of the army was away to escort the King's treasure transport. He sighed and silently cursed his King. His greed would cost many tax-payers lives, and the city would be a pillaged ruin very soon.

He had mustered his sorry bunch of soldiers, highly trained, but weary and wounded. Still, he saw determination in them. He knew many good men were lost today, and more would still go. The army of Goblins, Orcs and even a few giants would massacre them. "what has happened here?" he suddenly heard a deep, clear voice ask. the voice held great power in it, he could almost feel it. He turned around and saw an old man. despite his age, he seemed healthy, and his psoture was straight and proud. His gaze was strong, and his eyes held strength unlike any he had ever encountered. He wore simple robes and carried only with him a staff. Addams recognized the symbol on his robes, and knew this was a Protector. The group called itself Faith Protectors and helped the people all over the land vs great dangers.

Addams quickly explained their peril, hoping the Protector could help them somewhat. The old man told them to prepare, and await his signal. He entered the last remining tower and prepared for the onslaught. They heard his pryers and understood it was protections. He had some sort of divine radiance about him and his staff was blazing in flames. a few minutes later the top of the hill above the city became alive with foul creatures. the approached the city in a mad charge. When they closed on the city a wall of whirling blades appeared in front of them, ripping the front ranks to pieces. Acid rain started to fall on them seconds later, and howls of pain were heard all over the battlefield. Giving them no pause, columns of divine fires struck down from the heavens accompanied by hails of ice and stone. New walls of whirling blades of Death appeared amongst their broken ranks. they were disorganized and severely crippled. The Protector prepared his word of Faith to make ready for the soldiers to finish their enemy off.

One of the giants, badly wounded by the acid rain, realized the threat were from the man in the Tower and with a desperate feat of strength threw a huge boulder at him. The boulder crashed into the top of the tower and tore it apart. Addams saw the tower with the Protector crumbling to pieces and collapsing from the force of the boulder. His immediate thought was for the old man, but he had no time. his combat instincts kicked in and he yelled the order. "Charge!!! Press them into those blade walls! Siege crew, hit that giant!" The two remaining ballista's fired two huge spears which caught the giant in the chest, quickly folowed by dozens of arrows, each drawing a drop of blood. The giant fell backwards onto his comrades, and died shortly after.

Strengthened by the massive damage that had been done to their enemies, and fueled by hate, anger and fear the city soldiers quickly gained the upper hand on their disorganized foe and either killed them or pressed them into the protectors blade barriers. after the battle Addams sought out the ruined tower and managed to dig the old man out. He saw several of his bones were broken, but he could feel a faint breath. He called for the city healers to take care of him, perhaps he would survive.

The old man had saved the entire city and it's population. Addams knew he had to seek out the Faith Protectors and try to join their ranks. He owed the old man who unfaltering risked, and may still loose, his life for them.

A caster cleric, capable of combat as all clerics. Max WIS gives highest possible save DC vs evocation, and 6 DC away from max on other spheres. Gets a good AC and monk items availible. Remember not to use armor and shield once you access monk. You must wear armor up until then, remember to buff STR. You will be above dispel cap and hit toolset sr every time. When you are up against high sr monks they are usually not to hard in other aspects, buff up and hit them with arrows, if they hit you melee, perhaps Aura vs alignment helps. Cast both just in case.

He has okay hitpoints, especially buffed, and somewhat okay saves. You won't resist any dev crit and be careful to not get imploded. You'll be hard pressed to save against your own. If someone, f.ex. rogues or mages put up mantle's, cast Storm of Vengeance to eat 9 spell levels a round, and hope you manage to fire off implosion (or Word of Faith). I miss the Shield of Archons from 2nd ed. You should have summons ready also to distract IGMS and such, buying time for a vital 1 shot 1 kill spell.

I played with the thought of going with some CHA to get divine shield/ might to stay alive while bombing off spells. I also wondered about taking some expertise, but it is a bad exploit. Still possibly worthwhile.

Hope you enjoy my cleric. A dwarf would work with this build also. You loose skillpoints and a feat, perhaps combat casting, but gain improved saves and 40 hp's.
