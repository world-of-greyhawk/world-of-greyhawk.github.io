Edited By WhiZard on 03/14/09 20:08

Take Ranger pre-epic (will bump lvl 20 Wizard bonus feat into epic; take Greater Spell Focus with Ranger FE), and switch Harper Scout for Monk. You'll lose the HS bonus feat, but will get Evasion and can buff WIS to get some extra AC. Even better,if you were to use a little Melee Mage tactics with this build, you could dual-wield kamas w/ Flurry-of-Blows for 6 attacks, 7 hasted (!!) or 6 unarmed attacks w/ Flurry & Haste. Icing on the cake if you Melee Mage like this: you get Cleave for free, too.

Making those changes will loose you Epic Skill Focus (Discipline), but gain Evasion, Cleave, Improved Unarmed Strike, ability to use Monk robes/boots/gloves, and two extra main-hand attacks + option to Flurry when using kamas or unarmed. Essentially a Melee Mage that gets A LOT of mileage against the mobs with Flame Weapon + Keen Weapon + etc. kamas, and then can nuke the BBEG with 45DC spells vs. whatever its low save appears to be.

Incidentally, I don't find any Wizard/Ranger/Monk combos using pulse cap's search engine, so maybe it's never been included on this board. I've had this guy (without the tri-focus, and not a Dwarf; bravo on the tri-focus) sitting in a spreadsheet for more than a year, never play-tested.

On Edit: dropping Harper Scout will also allow you to switch Alertness and Iron Will for Extend Spell and Toughness.

On edit again: If you don't care about maxing Discipline, Tumble and Listen, then take the Monk level pre-epic, too. Level 17 would be the obvious candidate (though taking it earlier would only make getting Tumble 20 more expensive as Wizards can attain it by level 37 paying double, so the Monk tumble pre-epic is really just a discount). Doing so would make the build more playable early on, and would net you +2 to all saves. Moving Ranger pre-epic already gave you +2 Fort and -1 Will. This will cost you 20 points to Discipline, Tumble and Listen, maybe Persuade, too.
