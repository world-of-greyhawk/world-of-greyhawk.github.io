Nice build. And you get some extra points for style. If I may, I have a few suggestions which may or may not be helpful:

1. I belive you miscalculated your skill points. I think he actually gets 240. This gives pause, because now you have a choice. You can crank UMD up to 37, or you can skim from Concentration to crank Taunt up to 43. Personally, I prefer Taunting BGs.

2. Drop Strong Soul and LoH and take Power Attack and Cleave right away. Then, take Divine Might and Extra Turning when you qualify with your BG levels. This would truly maximize your charisma throughout the character's career. Your saves are still going to be solid. One eagles splendour and bardsong will see to that, plus your high spellcraft.

3. I would drop Epic Mage Armor. You'll have items at that point which will reduce the effectivenees EMA provides. In its place, I would consider Epic Skill Focus: Taunt, or a Great Strength. If you take the Great Strength, that would require you to drop Toughness for another Great Str. I would keep Lingering Song.

This would be a fun build to play, especially if you can get all the working parts to run at once, i.e., Bardsong, Curse Song, then hit opponent with Taunt, then launch attack with 2 stacking Bulls Strength with Divine Might and Power Attack turned on. Has the potential to do some nice damage.

By the way, nice name.

Ariel, Ookla, RIDE! personally, I won't take divine might/divine shield without at least 20 cha base. otherwise it doesn't last long enough to be useful.

I recommend getting blind-fight and knockdown if you can fit them someplace. Both are helpful against caster enemies. blind-fight for those that like to cast improved invis. knockdown to prevent them from casting altogether. knockdown will also help you land sneak attacks for a little damage boost.

I'd also consider leaving the palemaster levels for epic. going 12 bard/8 blackguard pre-epic gives you +1 bab. you won't get epic spells until later by doing that however. getting 12 bard also gives you +4 gmw pretty early, which will be useful if you're in a low/mid magic world for leveling. I hate to say it, but jjjhhhlll brings up another good point. However, assume you get your hands on a +5 Charisma Item, and assume then you cast Eagles Splendour for a +4 bonus. The Charisma is now 24, and for 7 rounds you can Wrath for an extra 7 Points of damage. Assume you also have reached 16BAB, and let's assume that, hasted, you land 3 out of 5 attacks per round. That's 21 extra points of damage per round for 7 rounds, for a combined 147 extra points of damage.

So, I don't think he'll need a 20 Base score to make it effective.

However, Blindfighting and Knockdown shouldn't be overlooked. Unfortunately, these are the dilemmas we face when we choose Bards and Blackguards. They each place feat demands on the player, and you must make your decision based upon you environment.

Ariel, Ookla, RIDE! yeah, it works with 16 base cha. with +12 from items/buffs he gets 9 rounds of 9 damage. Also consider that time spent casting shield/might is time not spent attacking, so every 10th round you lose all five attacks (so minus 81 avg damage from added damage (3*27)).

also consider that divine might is great against enemies with damage reduction and resistance, since it is extra damage that won't be resisted.

20 cha is my personal preference. I don't like having to re-cast divine might/divine shield all the time.
