I think it just means a multi-class character. If the build is Rogue/Fighter/Wizard with a strong emphasis on Rogue then it would be a Rogue hybrid. Hybrid, in this context, would mean that the character is more or less equally good at two or more 'classic' D&D roles... Like a Rogue/Fighter, or a Fighter/Mage, or a Mage/Cleric, or whatever.

For example, if you're a Wizard who takes one level of Rogue to skilldump some stuff into UMD and Tumble, you're not a Hybrid character. If you're a Wizard who can also sneak, disarm traps, do decent sneak attacks, etc. Then you're a hybrid character.
