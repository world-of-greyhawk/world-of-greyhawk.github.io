I see this build has been done to death, I want to add my version. Everybody says that cleric/rogue are very powerful and I wanted to test what I could take out of them).

For added cheese I did put a single monk level in the build in order to get wisdom AC bonus (It has been so hard to avoid the temptation of a dual kama wielder...).

This is a fairly explosive build, he is not really good when he is unbuffed but he can become awesome once he self buffs himself he can reach
49 AC with plain clothes (self buffed and dualwielding)
58 AB with mundane dual daggers and 1d4 +28 +1d6 +[12d6] damage per hit with 3(4 if hasted) attacks per round at full BAB.

He also has epic dodge and can cast improved invisibility (50% concealment) and can reach really good AC.

He can cast anything from scrolls (I recommend finding premonition, summon spells, keen edge, flame weapon, mage armor, cat's grace as a basic set).

He can summon, use darkness, hammer of the gods... to land his 12d6 sneak attacks (with crippling strike) on his victim.

Some of his buffs wont last long, but with the right planning he can take down almost anything in matter of seconds.

Tactics:

This one is the most effective against non-true vision enemies:
Use your long term buffs on you, set up a "battlefield" with area spells (Darkness + blade barriers + creeping doom), cast your short term buffs and lure your victims to the darkness.

Use a summon. Uses to work good... but for very short time.
Find a tank and get him in the frontline while you safely backstab things to death.


Cons:
If you get dispelled you are dead.
I dont recommend playing this one on a world with high rest restrictions nor lots of creatures with true vision.
