Keep Spellcraft and Concentration maxed and use the extra points for Heal, Lore and Tumble.

At 40th Lvl Use Divine Shield in combat to boost AC... at 40th lvl that should add +10 AC with an empowered Eagle's Splendour that should go up to... between 11 and 13. Wearing Full Plate should add another +8, using a Tower Shield should add another 3 plus the bonus from a near tp maxed cross class tumble(20)+4 making the basic bonus without wasting any spell other than Eagles Splendour

10 +11(13)+ 8 + 3 + 4 = 36. (tack on Epic Mage Armour and epic warding and you will have ac 56 with a dr of 50/+20 for 26 rounds.)

This can easily be boosted by further enhancing damage resistance and better equpment... so it is forseeable that by 33rd Level you should have a character who has a reasonably high AC able to cast epic spells without fear of miscast.

For the Sorcerers familiar I took the sprite for the utility value of her however if you feel you need more combat feel free to take something else

Anyways the idea was to take the maximum advantage from the character's high Charisma as possible using classes that take advantage of the high Charisma. Whilst still maintaining a strong spell casting character who won't fall over in combat too easily.

So that is the idea I had.. deliberately avoiding PM and RDD (as this was also complained about)to hopefully produce a well rounded combat caster.
