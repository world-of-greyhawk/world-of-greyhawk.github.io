Playable 1 - 40 PvM

In troll dens and ogre camps, scarred brutes gather near campfires in the dark nights of winter to share tales of adventures past. Many of these tales feature beautiful human women who cut down troll and ogre comrades with flashing swords or strumming longbows, both weapons wielded with equal deadly efficiency.
When the sun goes abed early and the cold winds blow daggers of ice through skin and cloak, bandits and ne'er-do-wells muster together in dim, smoky taverns to retell of past encounters. Many of their stories have skirmishes with the same ferocious sword-wielding and bow-wielding women.
Across the lands, all who follow dark paths lower their voices and look about cautiously when speaking of the terrifying Swordmaidens of Torm...

The idea behind this build was to put together a solid sword-wielder who could also wield a bow with skill. Although some may say an Elf would make a better sword & bow fighter, I wanted to stay close to the idea of the Norse Valkyrie so I chose Human.

This character is not only a strong straightforward fighter, but she has a few tricks up her sleeves:

  • Champion of Torm levels yielding +12 to all saves
  • Bard Song for +2 Attack, +2 Dmg, +1 All Saves, +8 HP, +1 Skill Bonus
  • Use Magic Device skill
  • Tumble skill (40) yielding +8 to AC
  • Taunt skill which can reduce an opponent's AC by 6 and cause spell failure of 30%
  • Called Shot feat which can either whittle down an opponent's strength, or slow down their movement speed, permitting an archer to gain more shots on-goal (Yes, "on-goal"! Forget duck season - it's hockey season!)
  • Epic Weapon Specialization in both Longsword and Longbow, adding total of +6 damage to each strike
  • Improved Knockdown to keep opponents down and unable to fight back
  • Improved Power Attack to add 10 points of additional damage to easy-to-hit opponents
  • Rapid Shot and Point Blank Shot to aid her archery


Customizations one could make are to move points from Strength to Charisma, permitting some Bardic spellcasting as well as improving some CoT abilities. And of course, one could shuffle points between Strength and Dexterity as they like, to improve one attack over the other. I opted to focus on Strength and swordfighting, and make archery secondary. (Although one idea I had was to go Dex-based to empower the archery skills, and use Weapon Master levels to boost the Longsword attack. Perhaps that build will come another day...)

Suggestions, questions, and criticisms welcome.
