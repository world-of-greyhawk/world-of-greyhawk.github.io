Back with another dex build, I wanted to make something special this time.
What about a scary little savage that appears out of nowhere to tear apart all who get in his way?
A protector of his tribe, this savage little thing is not the usual halfling you are accustomed to... definitely not!
Sure you could easily go elf with this build (wild elves are there for that, no?) but that would take all the fun out of playing this little mean thing
A short story is coming too...
Here we go now:

Advantages

1) Good AB, even ranged. You can hips, hit from afar then relocate and hit again if you want

2) Decent saves (will apart)

3) Good naked AC. Get yourself some nice robes, bracers, amulets, rings to boost it

4) Epic dodge and SC5: you won't get hit often

5) Hips, uncanny dodge, improved evasion, slippery mind, defensive roll, shadow evade 12/+4, shadow daze, summon shadow, darkvision: the full shadow dancer package!

6) Greater rage and terrifying rage. Walk unseen among your enemies and then rage and scare them off...at that point they are dead meat

7) Maximized taunt: up to -6 AC + 30% spell failure to your foes


Disadvantages

1) Low will save, against spells it gets better...luckily there's slippery mind to help

2) no UMD: no way to wear those nice monk robes ... too bad SD don't get the skill!

3) a little tight on feats: no epic shadow lord (altough you can drop EWS and swapping the last fighter lvl with a SD lvl you can get it)

4) one level short of shadow evade 14/+5

5) mediocre HP

6) mediocre damage output


Possible changes to the build are choosing elf instead of halfling, dropping dual wield and going sword (rapier?) and board (this frees up 3 feats pre-epic: maybe LoH, iron will and toughness?), dropping EWS for Epic shadow lord.
I would have liked to get the 20th SD lvl for the increase in shadow evade but I couldn't find a way to do it without having to drop either terrifying rage either WS/EWS.

Comments are of course welcome as any suggestions on how to improve the build.

It's SHADOWDANCER, dammit, shadowdancer!!! Heh, I've built a few of these feral-eyed freaks. You did a fairly decent job at putting this guy together, good job. I have only one suggestion, and you can take it with a grain of salt.

Rogue over Shadowdancer. Perhaps the build's biggest drawback is damage output and lack of UMD. While you're tough defensively, you lack any real offensive threat.

You could go Barbarian16/Rogue24 (Barb15/Rogue25), still get enough bonus feats to get Epic Dodge and SC V, obtain more skill points, grab Terrifying Rage at Lvl24, still be able to top out Intimidate at the end via Rogue class skill, get 13d6 sneak attack (91d6 per round hasted) and avoid an Experience point penalty. You'll further have a great UMD score, top out all your Trap skills, and have an excellent way to generate sneaks through your Terrifying Rage.
