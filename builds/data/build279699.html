Sneaking, Trapping and Assassinating Enemies PvP and PvM (1-40)

NIGHT TALON
By Deborah “Tyr” Templar


Concept:

The PC default biography on Halflings reads, "You may be short, but you're quick and full of spirit. You've recently wandered into human settlements, eager to see what you can learn from the tall-folk." But this isn’t your true story; while you did enter a human settlement, it was in truth for the purpose of exacting revenge against the humans responsible for killing a member of your family, a beloved merchant held up and killed by some highway robbers whilst en route, which robbers you tracked down and in turn killed, but you were caught. Impressed with your skills, however, Faerun’s powers-that-be gave you a choice: a death sentence for murder in cold-blood, or life as an assassin. You chose the latter.

Pros:

• Very nice AB. Night Talon has a 44 AB (+2 cat’s/-2 dual-wielding), which is particularly good on an assassin-heavy build.
• Very good AC (“monk like” with a shield) with Uncanny Dodge and Impr.Invis.
• Good HPs (over 400 HPs, which is also really nice on an assassin-heavy build)
• Very nice Death Attack DC
• High Sneak Damage (i.e., 14d6 sneak die (that’s 84d6 sneak/round (84d7 hasted), being wonderful to slice through enemies (incl. DR enemies), with HiPs/stealth for generating attacks
• A nice skillset, including full UMD, good discipline (on par with a str-based warrior actually, and really nice to have on a dex-based build), and also deadly trap skills
• Wonderful synergies/efficiencies between the classes: Ranger for BAB/AB (4 apr / 6 apr dual / 7 hasted), with complementary skills/stealth (bolstered by Ranger and Assassin spells, incl. impr.invisibility and darkness from Assassin, with ranger bringing in ultravision), and SD for evasion, HiPs (which comes nice and early, too).


Cons:

• Fort could be better, but reflex is high (and coupled with evasion), will save is low, and you will want to get clarity potions and/or put your UMD to good use and shore it up
• Low damage vs. the sneak immune (i.e., undead, constructs), being typical of dexer-sneakers, and no impr.crit (since sneak doesn’t multiply on a crit. anyway), but offset by UMD and deadly traps (thar be damage)
• Slightly feat-starved and no ED, but in light of the above (HPs, HiPs, UMD, skills incl. traps, discipline, AB and AC, etc.), has all s/he really needs, making ED unnecessary.
