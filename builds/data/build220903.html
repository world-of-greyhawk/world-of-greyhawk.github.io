The Master of the Deathaxe is a skilled warrior who can stand his ground, completely ignoring lesser wounds (EDR III) and cutting down his enemies with skilled and painful precision (46 AB, WS/EWS). He can move in the midst of combat in heavy plate and tower shield without causing AoO and his acrobatic skills make him harder to hit together with his resiliant, hardened skin (maxed out tumble and AS).
He knows where to hit to really hurt (5d6 death attack DC 22), has access to all scrolls and magical objects he can put his hands on (maxed UMD), plus he gets improved invisibility for free due to his assassin training.
One of his main concern are mind affecting spells against which he should try to find the best protection possible.

I built him with Greataxe and he's fun. Hacks through 15/- DR like it wasn't there I managed to land most of my IKDs, too - even against a Strength-based RDD.

The Rogue version loses some of the general sexyness of the Assassin class but is - as usual, which is truly a shame - a better choice. I would go with Crippling Strike but that's just personal preference. Some people will enjoy the defensive boost that Improved Evasion gives. Spamming Knockdown with Crippling Strike is a sure way to fame, however When you have landed enough Crippling Strikes your opponent will get -3 Damage from their Strength (makes your 9/- DR feel like Epic Warding for the opponent) and will most likely be encumbered.
