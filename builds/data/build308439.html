The young skald was on a well-earned break and had ordered some drink for herself. She took her tankard, and one more, and went to a table where an aging man was seated. She had noticed the man during her performance, and wanted his story. The man wore an old plate mail and an old shield was located beside his chair. Both were battered and showed signs of great usage in battle. Beside him on the bench, with it's point down, he had put his sword. Unlike the armour however, the sword was spotless, seemingly unused and even felt almost radiant. "May I sit, honorable warrior?" the skald asked. "Oh, please sit. Some company won't hurt an old man, especially from a beautiful lady." he replied and gestured to the bench opposite his table. The skald sat down and gave the warrior the other tankard. They exchanged a handshake and their names. "I want your story, venerable warrior." the skald said "You look for sure like a great warrior, and my guess is you have an interesting story in all those scars of yours.". She noticed that aside from his scars and his clear signs of advanced age, that he was in quite good shape. His eyes were clear and strong still, and had that warmth in them that you see only in the rarest of persons. She also noticed that although he was still somewhat big, his armour was a little loose. She could imagine how those arms had once been strong as oak. That chest as strong as castle walls. "I was, well am, a Paladin, Milady. And seeing how old I've become I like to think I was good at what I did." The old man gratefully sipped his tankard while he studied this young skald. She had the immortality of youth almost seeping out, and her gaze was always seeking, curious, studying. Although it's magic is tied to me, and it seems to have waned somewhat." He sighed again, his look somewhat dreamy and distant. She listened intently, and took some notes. FP+Board with no extra source of AC. Not much hitpoints, below average for a full bred warrior.

Pro's? Oh yeah, he has that too. Melee offense. Excellent AB, with his taunt being succesful it might hit an effective 75. And the damage, although no Bane, is decent enough. Seeing the numerous crits you'll get with keen longsword damage is quite sufficient. In times long gone now, I played this on the dragonlance server (which has just returned) and I tell you, this guy ripped melee builds apart. With a true seeing item he ripped most things apart. Sorry Avado, but combat clerics were just a quick walk in the park. Taunt is a pain, but in most cases I didn't need it, or fired it after a succesful KD, or when the other guy did some casting or whatever. KD.

That's way above any cleric and above even RDDs.
