Playable from levels 1-40... PvM build

Hopefully this build will be popular with the Dwarf fans. One of the big problems with Most Dwarven Defenders is the Low AB, so I set out to make a Dwarven Defender that had a good AB by adding Weapon Master Levels. The end result is a good tank build; it isn't pretty, it doesn't have any tricks up its sleeves, but it can take a hell of a pounding and dish one out as well. This build works the best in a low to mid magic setting where devastating critical is banned. Preferably he can be part of a party where his weaknesses can be compensated for by other party members. Oddly enough, with mind spell protection he makes a good scout. Not because he's sneaky, but because very few things can kill him before he can retreat.

There are a few similar builds, including one by Rooker1963 that has the same level distribution. This build does not have devasating critical and either has a higher AB or a higher DR than the other DwD/WM builds. When you throw in the little perks like improved knockdown and blind-fight, I felt that this build was unique enough to post (had I searched for this level combination before making the build I probably would not have built it). Whether it is better or worse than the other builds I will leave to the community to decide.

Advantages

-High AB. A 45 AB is pretty good for a Dwarven Defender.
-17-20 critical threat range on an X4 multiplier bludgeoning weapon. The 100+ point critical hits are almost as good as devastating critical.
-18 points of damage reduction and over 700 hit points. He can take a good deal of punishment. Watching a dex build try to take him down is entertaining.
-Good Fortitude Save. He should be safe against most Death Magic.

Disadvantages

-No spell casting ability. Melee is his only form of attack.
-Mediocre reflex and low will save. The Reflex save isn't that bad. If he fails his save he takes damage and he can absorb a good deal of that. The will save can be problematic, hopefully he can find some way to defend himself from mind spells.

It should be so, but "the rapier of the high road (keen rapier)" is avaliable in HotU as the default loot from the ogre mage in Halasters dungeon. There is supposed to be blunt property that has the same effect as keen. But the NWN developers, being computer programmers and ipso facto lazy (Honestly, have you ever met a computer programmer that wasn't a little lazy? Must be a personality trait of the people attracted to that profession, or a side effect of spending all that time infront of a computer.), only implemented the keen property but it applies to all weapons. The keen edge spell only works on slashing weapons, as it should. The early rapiers, which I suppose the D&D rapiers are, would have been heavier than what we think of as a rapier* and would have had a cutting edge so it should probably be a slashing/piercing weapon, hence the keen edge spell should probably work on it.

*=Fencing had not yet evolved and the war swords of the time were still heavy enough to breach armor, so most sword training would have favored a heavy swing. Metallurgy, though it had improved due to the Islamic techniques gained from the Crusades, would also have dictated a heavier blade. The blade would still have probably have been light enough to break if tried to parry the heavier blades (IIRC, the Bastard sword was still being used when the Rapier was first developed). The blade also would have been straight instead of the silly curved NWN models.
