SILVANITE SHADEWOOD MISTRESS


The Forest Father attracts from all corners of the globe: those with a deep connection with nature. The Church of Silvanus has defended woods and forests from the encroachment of civilization for many thousands of years; and the war continues behind the scenes.

Having accumulated a great deal wisdom from her adventures, a lone Silvanite druid reasons that deception is essential in the ongoing secret war between silvanites and the industrious. An old and anonymous shadowdancer introduces her to the way of the shadow...

"...where there is light, there is a shadow...where there is no light, there exists a myriad of shadows...but even the most infinitesimal of shadows can hide a mountain..."

Being wise and young, she learns quickly. The shadows bend to her will...they shield her...encompass her...even as she summons ancient draconic spirits to mold her body into that of a thousand year old dragon, the shadows blend and adapt...

The Church of Silvanus counted her amongst the greatest of their assets...she blended into the shadows like the oaks of Shadewood...she became the Shadewood Mistress.



...essentially she's a HIPsing druid/dragon. This is more of an in-depth guide (and advertisement) for a generic dragon build. Hard to come up with something original for a seven year old game...

You wouldn't want to be caught by both stonehold and storm of vengeance; even if you're immune to paralysis, she can cast harm on you as you enter the storm...'nuff said.

Her Pros
Moan in agony as a building-sized dinosaur with a wingspan the size of an A380 disappears into the crimson sunset...even the best spotters and listeners cannot detect this girl!

A naked AC of 76 in Dragon Form ensures that only those with the craziest of ABs can hit her when she actually equips something.

'Spell Resistance' Buff gives 48 spell resistance; casters without at least 30 caster levels shouldn't even try...

Conversely, she has loads of spell penetration from her 36 druid levels...

Kinda hard to dispel her when she have 36 druid levels. Yup, even exalted sorcerer will have some trouble!

She has spell DCs for conjuration type spells that rival even the exalted sorcerer's spell DCs...(DC 43)

40/+6 naked damage reduction along with all the other dragon benefits = GG in low magic world

Her dragon's breath attack is surprisingly effective when all else fails.

Those druid levels give her +4 to hide and move silently and +2 to attack rolls when she is in a wilderness area...run Tarzan, run!



Her cons
True seeing...gah...good thing it's not constant and immediate, so you can still take advantage of flatfoots...

Watch out for the exalted sorcerer, his spells CAN touch her, she's lucky to have death ward and some really good saves against spells...

She did not take spell penetration feats...mainly because she wants to resist her own spells, but 45 spell resistance is the 50/50 mark for her...

If a dispel actually connects, she can't recast her buffs in dragon shape...

She is a dragon. She will not fit into a corridor. Then again, she can be your 300 spartans because she's so big (^_^)

The thing about dragon builds like her is that they don't benefit very much from items...While she is beyond godlike in a low magic world, she's not particularly overwhelming in high magic worlds.

Other dragon builds achieve higher AC and higher AB; though it would be a long battle, she will ultimately lose one-on-one. well, I guess she can unmorph and cast Storm of Vengeance and then cast harm on that dragon...

She is not immune to critical hits; watch out for devastating critical! Especially from Clerics.


Strategies
Cast Stonehold, cast Storm of Vengeance and then cast harm...great for assassinating heavily armed warriors

Cast Maximized Inferno, hide, run...not a great killer but a great pisser-offer, doesn't look like the effect can be removed

Cast Stonehold, Cast Icestorm...good against a group of brutes with low spell resistance and low will save

Morph into Dragon...ultimately, this is your favorite strategy, especially against those with high spell resistance and immunity to paralysis and all that jazz...use your hide creatively

Potions are your best friend...you can use them in dragon shape...full heal with an already hard to hit dragon who can also hide, that's something.

Prismatic Dragons have their AC made up mostly of natural AC and so I suspect Dracoliches too, hence why you hit them very easily with a touch attack.
