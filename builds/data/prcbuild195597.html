The Singing Dragon (Bard 20/RDD 10/Initiate of Draconic Mysteries 10)

When my first ever character (a halforc Barbarian/Bard/RDD) got the Great Wyrm Gauntlets and discovered they were entirely useless to her I was quite annoyed. This build was designed to achieve 2 things :
1)Allow a Bard/RDD to melee unarmed effectively without taking Monk levels.
2)Allow a RDD to actually become a Dragon, something I always thought would be fun.

The spell list is based on her going solo and having no magic items.
Due to her low Spell DC I found buffs and those few spells that have no saves (like Legion’s Curse of Impending Blades and Ice Storm) most useful.

Magic Item Wishlist: Robes of the Dark Moon (or Drow Spellchain and Boots of Haste), Great Wyrm Gauntlets or a good pair of Monk gloves, a big nasty 2-hander for fighting Crit Immune and/or high DR creatures, and a STR enhancing item to make those criticals she does manage to land count.

Pros
Dragon form at level 22
Devastating Critical at level 24
Good saves with the +9 from Spellcraft
Improved Evasion and Fire Immunity
With all her buffing spells up and level 16 Bard and Curse Song going she is quite impressive.

Cons
Only 3 attacks/round
Doesn’t critical very often
SR is helpful when she gets it but by level 40 is pretty useless
Vulnerable to having her buffs dispelled

Variations
Toughness is a pretty optional feat. I also considered Blind Fighting, Extend Spell and Lingering Song.
A human version can be made. Losing +6 STR hurts but the extra feat and skills help. If the human took 8 levels of RDD pre-epic and the last 2 at level 21-22 it could take the same feats at the same levels but wouldn’t get Dragon form until level 28.
