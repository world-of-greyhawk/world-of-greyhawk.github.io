Karesh crept forward, senses straining, eyes focused on the hunting camp in the hollow before him. His right hand gripped the shaft of his great axe, it's long, scarred shaft giving off sheen from the moonlight. He glanced at the weapon - how many times had he gone into battle with his mighty fists gripping that axe? How many foes had fallen before his fierce skill with the blade? He gave a cold chuckle as the countless faces of his victims briefly passed before him. But then he grunted and shook the shaggy black mane from his face as he remembered why he was here. Why his Wolfsfang clansmen had ridden with him for a week, nearly killing their horses as they rode fast and far from their homes. Three of his men had fallen to the giant steppe wolves, screaming as their throats were torn out. It was all in pursuit of her: the one known as the The Siren. Karesh paused as his mind shot back to the day that triggered this hunt...

It was on a routine patrol of his clan's borders when there had been a clash at a river ford. Karesh and his men had surprised a group of enemy riders breaking camp. The Wolfsfang riders rode down upon the surprised intruders, expecting an easy skirmish. But events turned out in a different manner and Karesh went down, an arrow in his shoulder. By the time he got to his feet, cursing the odds, the fight was over. His clan had won, but had paid a deadly price. Four men were mortally wounded - one was his younger brother. A handful of the opponents had managed to escape, among them their leader, The Siren. That day Karesh swore to avenge his brother's death by one day mounting the head of The Siren on a spear in his camp and parading it for all to see.

He snapped back to the present as he heard his lieutenant hissing behind him, wondering why the delay? Karesh curtly signaled to have his men sweep in and to commence the attack.

The Wolsfang men crept down towards The Siren's camp slowly and stealthily. A horse neighed and stomped somewhere in the camp. Karesh glanced around, wary now. His men were close enough for the final charge, for the bloodthirsty screams that would give an advantage over the sleeping, unsuspecting victims. He grinned, a fierce wolf's grin. He glanced around to make sure all was as it should be, stood, raised his axe and drew in a great breath to give the shout that would trigger the attack.

But suddenly the quiet embers of the campfire erupted in a great blaze and a chilling cry roared from a shadowy figure behind the fire. Karesh fell back chocking and blinking fiercely at the bright light. By the old Gods! It was her! The Siren was prepared for them and was springing a trap on them! As her cries washed over the attackers they seemed to reverberate back from the walls of the hollow. She stepped forward into the glow of the fire, clad in the armor showing her ties to the southern cities, moonlight and firelight flickering off the surface of her plate mail and tower shield in a macabre crimson and silver dance. She began chanting and her axe lit up with an arcane aura. Her voice shifted to an uncanny wail and Karesh felt the hairs on his neck rise and he felt his courage leak away. Suddenly he felt a wet warmth trickle down his legs and thought briefly he had taken another arrow, a mortal shot from which he would slowly and painfully bleed to death. Then he realized that it wasn't blood running down his legs. As he gathered his tattered will and raised his axe, he wryly thought at least his clan would never know he had wet his britches in battle and no dishonor would fall on his family. Then he swallowed grimly and stepped down to meet The Siren and the end of his days.
