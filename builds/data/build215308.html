Arut was awfully scared. It was not the fact that he was a small boy in a scary place which frightened him so much, as it was the fact that he was small body in a scary place filled with hungry undead. But all of these things mattered little to him, since his head was filled with mind-numbing visions of his own death, coming towards him slowly. Why he had been playing in the old forest he couldn't exactly remember, but again, he was awfully scared. If he had remembered, he would have learned that listening to older boys brag and trying to match them is not a good idea.

As the fiends moved in on him, he though he heard a muffled noises, followed by shadows moving behind the ghouls infront of him. He closed his eyes, awaiting his death. A great set of hands laid a hold of him, and lifted him into the air. He screamed. The final bite never came. He found himself, outside, sitting in the middle of a clearing in the woods, what little sun there was left in the day shining on him. He was no longer surrounded by monsters, but now by grim men and women. They were busy, cleaning their axes, flails and swords. Dressed in strangely dull armour, their cloth looked poor, yet there was something almost divine about this small group. One of them, the nearest, a woman, turned to Arut, and spoke.

"You were lucky, little one", she intoned in a quiet yet stern voice. "Another moment and you would have been another lost child and lunch in the bellies of many."

Arut shuddered. He wondered why he felt so safe amidst a group of such mean and dark strangers. Many wore hoods, hiding in vain the scars and injuries many of them shared.

"My name is Arut. Are you going to hurt me?"

"No, Arut. We will not hurt you. But there are those things which do hurt the innocent. It is our chosen path to defend against such things. What do you think of that?" asked the same woman. For the first time Arut noticed how tall she was.

"I think that is something which is needed."

"And would you choose such a path yourself, to face darkness, have it press against your very soul, and risk life or limb for another?" Her voice seemed to waiver as she mentioned the darkness.

"I don't know. I am too small to fight. But I want to help others."

The dark figures around him stopped what they were doing. They all looked at him. The few without hoods gave him a sort of smile, as if acknowledging him for the first time. After a pause, they set back to sorting out their gear, and the pile of items scavenged from the ancient barrows below them.

"That is all that any can do. Come, you will stay with us, if that is your wish. Among us Ranging Slayers you will learn the arts of combat. You will grow bigger, stronger, and with the help of Torm, you will come to battle the darkness, too. You will develop what seems impossible. Your mastery will begin with yourself and none know where it will end. This path is dark, filled with peril, but so it must be, to protect those who live in the light. What do you think of that?" The eyes of the tall, dark woman grew keen, seeming able to search Arut's soul with but a glance into his eyes.

"I would like to learn." Arut replied.

The lady smiled. Taking Arut by the hand, she led him off to the pile of equipment taken from the barrow. Arut's training was about to begin.

"You cannot pass!" Nice story Christian. I like the build as well. Just a suggestion that might increase the playability by a notch:

Take EWF as your level 21 feat. That will immediately increase your AB by 2, while the GRT STR does nothing for AB and damage until level 24, where your STR point raises STR to 22.

For your CoT bonus feat at level 22, take either Armor Skin (for the +2 AC right away) or Epic Prowess (for the +1 AB boost right away). Whichever one you don't take here, take at level 30.

At level 24, take your GRT STR I feat. This will directly jump your STR to 22 with the STR ability increase at level 24 and pay immediate dividends in AB and damage.
